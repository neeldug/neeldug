Hi! This is my first official blog post. So, over the past Summer, I've been learning [Rust](https://www.rust-lang.org/)! Rust's a wonderful systems language, that's taught me a lot that my brief course in C++ didn't cover.

I think I'll quickly cover why I love Rust, and why you might do too!

- Tooling! Rust's tooling is incredible, I've worked with Node, Meteor, Python, but Rust excels here. cargo is brilliant with package management, benchmarking, formatting and even has `clippy` to help with guidance.
- The compiler errors, having worked with C++ for a long time, Rust is so refreshing in comparison, long gone are the days having to stare at SegFaults and being clueless what's happening, Rust gives you everything, where the issue arises, how to fix it, further information on the error, the list goes on and on.
- The community! This is a big one, while I'm sure many of you know the Stack Overflow community's support for Rust, I've personally seen how wonderful the Rust Development team is when it comes to newcomers, there's Rustlings, and even issues that you can raise for clarification on anything!

I have spent the past month or two making minor contributions to [boa](https://github.com/boa-dev/boa/), an open-source JavaScript lexer, parser and compiler built in Rust. In working on a few PR's on this project, I've learnt how to properly structure commits and general DevOps protocols.

Finally, before I end off this ramble, I wanted to highlight how awesome CI, or Continuous Integration, is. It's actually so cool, I learnt about CI through Boa and other open-source projects, where almost anything can be automated, whether it be unit testing on PR's and Pushes, benchmarking, generating coverage reports, anything you can automate, it can do!
